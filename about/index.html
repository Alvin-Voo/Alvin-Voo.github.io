About

I am interested in the human condition.

Especially on the restlessness of human beings, which can be characterized as:

  1. Neuroticism (Emotional Instability)
  2. Wanting to one up each other (Competition)
  3. The unsettling attitude, always looking for something ‘better’ (a.k.a *the grass is always greener somewhere else)

Humans are prey animals, who gradually learned their way to become predators throughout the millennia. Adding to the fact that we are self-conscious (i.e. we know we will die some day), this explains why our fight-or-flight circuitry seems to be always on or at least have a baseline which is a few notches higher than the rest of the animals. After all, we are on top of the food chain.

Eastern philosophy teaches us to dial ourselves down a bit.

Nevertheless, the advancement in technology and the explosion of human population since the last few decades, have made this quite the challenge. The former seems to be the ultimate reason for humans’ overall decreased in attention span; while the latter has resulted in a worldwide skill competition.

This blog shall explore some aspects of the aforementioned, in most cases starting from the author himself.

*The Grass is NOT always greener
